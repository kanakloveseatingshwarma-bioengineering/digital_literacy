# Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud Targeting a College Student
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India right now, especially among young people who use apps like GPay, PhonePe, and Paytm regularly.
Here is how it typically happens, step by step. First, the attacker finds their target — usually through OLX, Instagram, or WhatsApp — and contacts them pretending to be a buyer, seller, or someone who needs to send money urgently. They then send a collect request on UPI, which looks like an incoming payment. The victim, thinking they are about to receive money, enters their UPI PIN to "accept" it. The moment they do, money is deducted from their account instead.
College students are a prime target because they frequently buy and sell second-hand items online, are new to UPI, and may not know that receiving money never requires entering a PIN.
The consequences can be devastating — students have lost anywhere from a few hundred to several thousands of rupees in a matter of seconds. Beyond financial loss, victims also experience anxiety, embarrassment, and a loss of trust in digital payment systems.
A realistic scenario: A student posts a second-hand laptop for sale on OLX. A "buyer" contacts them, sends a collect request saying "I'm sending you the advance payment," and the student unknowingly approves it and loses money.
Source: Based on fraud patterns reported on cybercrime.gov.in
